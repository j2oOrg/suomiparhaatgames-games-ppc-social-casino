Two years in the making

A longer look at how the three-game set and the studio came together.

We registered the company after late-night project sessions near the harbor. We were swapping playlists and game links, and most sites felt loud.

The name sounded warm and local, so it stuck. Early feedback was simple: keep it honest.

Year one was Harbor Dice and Aurora Match. We kept the motion readable and the pace slow enough to pause.

Year two brought Reel Drift, cleaner navigation, and tighter copy.

The point is short sessions. Dice is the simplest, matching takes more focus, and reels sit in the middle.
